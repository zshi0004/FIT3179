The Housing Market of The South-Eastern Melbourne

It has be stated in the media recently that the housing market of Melbourne has finally started cooling off.
However, is this the REAL case?

Background Information

Study the data of property sales from 2017 to 2018 of the south-eastern metropolitan has found the number of property sold and the areal price per bedroom (PPB) decreased dramatically from 2017 to 2018.
Areal PPB has decreased by almost 20,000 dollar.


Is this TRUE for each suburb?

Let's compare the price per bedroom of each suburb to that of the area.
It is found that more PPB of a suburb is greater than the areal PPB for the whole south-eastern Melbourne metropolitan. There are less dark reddish areas on the map of 2018.
Suburbs like Clayton and Dandenong have significantly increased their PPB in 2018.
It can be inferred the cooling-off of the Melbourne housing market may not be true for every suburb in the south-eastern area.

Let's Have A More Closer Look At The Monash City Council...

Did you spot that the colour for most suburbs belonging to the Monash City council changed to a bluish colour?
It's not possible to ignore the colour of Clayton which becomes the darkest blue on the map for 2018 from a salmon colour in 2017.
Though the number of properties sold in a year has decreased for all suburbs in 2018, it can be found on the right bubble plot, the PPB for most suburbs has passed the areal PPB of 2018.
The only three suburb with PPB lower than the areal PPB is Hughesdale, Oakleigh South and Mulgrave.
